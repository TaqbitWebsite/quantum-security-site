How our products leverage quantum physics to provide unprecedented security

DPR QKD: Coherent One-Way & Differential Phase Shift

Our Distributed Phase Reference (DPR) Quantum Key Distribution systems include implementations of both the Coherent One-Way (COW) and Differential Phase Shift (DPS) protocols. These protocols encode key information across multiple pulses, leveraging phase coherence rather than single-photon encoding.

The security relies on quantum principles such as the No-Cloning Theorem and the disturbance of quantum coherence during interception. Any eavesdropping attempt introduces detectable anomalies in the interference pattern or timing sequence, ensuring robust security even in high-loss environments.

  • • Decoy Sequences (COW): Random decoy pulses are used to monitor coherence and detect photon number splitting attacks.
  • • Phase Difference Encoding (DPS): Each bit is encoded in the phase shift between successive pulses, providing natural resistance to intercept-resend attacks.
  • • High-Speed Transmission: Continuous wave lasers and interferometric detection enable key generation rates in the multi-KHz range.
  • • Interference Monitoring: Real-time monitoring of phase coherence ensures immediate detection of any eavesdropping attempt.

QRNG: True Quantum Randomness

Our Quantum Random Number Generators exploit the nature of fundamental unpredictability of quantumness.It uses single-photon detection where the randomness comes from the quantum uncertainty in photon arrival times.

Unlike pseudo-random number generators (PRNGs) or hardware random number generators (HRNGs) that rely on classical noise sources, our QRNGs provide randomness that is certified by quantum physics to be fundamentally unpredictable.
