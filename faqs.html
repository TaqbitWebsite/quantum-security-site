Security Questions

How soon should organizations transition to quantum-safe cryptography?

The transition to quantum-safe cryptography should begin immediately for most organizations handling sensitive data with long-term confidentiality requirements. NIST recommends starting migration planning now, with full implementation within 5-10 years. However, organizations in finance, healthcare, government, and critical infrastructure should prioritize this transition due to the "harvest now, decrypt later" threat.

What's the difference between QKD and post-quantum cryptography?

Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) uses quantum physics principles to securely distribute encryption keys, providing information-theoretic security. Post-quantum cryptography refers to classical cryptographic algorithms that are resistant to quantum computer attacks. While QKD offers stronger security guarantees, post-quantum cryptography is often easier to deploy. Many organizations implement both for defense-in-depth quantum security.
